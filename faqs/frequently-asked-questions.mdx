## General

<ExpandableGroup>
  <Expandable title="How quickly do I see results after connecting a source?" default-open="true">
    Discovery and classification start automatically after the first successful connection, so the first findings usually appear within hours, not weeks.
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="How is ARMOR different from a traditional DSPM tool?" default-open="false">
    Traditional tools stop at discovery. ARMOR adds the Semantic Triad and a Sensitivity plus Exposure risk model, so findings are ranked by business impact rather than raw count.
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Does ARMOR store copies of my documents?" default-open="false">
    No. Connectors extract and normalize content for inference. ARMOR keeps labels, metadata, and risk scores, not full document copies. See [Security, Privacy & Compliance](/technical-architecture-security-and-privacy/security-privacy-and-compliance).
  </Expandable>
</ExpandableGroup>

## Classification and risk

<ExpandableGroup>
  <Expandable title="What is the Semantic Triad?" default-open="false">
    It is the combination of Content, Context, and Intent that ARMOR uses to classify an asset. See [AI-Based Document Classification](/fundamentals-and-functionality/fundamentals/ai-based-document-classification).
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Why is a low-sensitivity file marked Critical?" default-open="false">
    Risk combines Sensitivity with Exposure. A moderately sensitive file that is broadly reachable can outrank a highly sensitive file that is locked down. See [Understanding Risk](/fundamentals-and-functionality/fundamentals/understanding-risk-sensitivity-and-exposure).
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Can I correct a wrong classification?" default-open="false">
    Yes. Adjust category alignment or the confidence threshold, and affected assets are re-scored. Only affected assets are re-evaluated.
  </Expandable>
</ExpandableGroup>
