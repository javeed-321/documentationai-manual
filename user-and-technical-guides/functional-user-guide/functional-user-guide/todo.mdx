## Overview

This guide is for the people who work findings every day. It shows how to read the risk dashboard, triage a finding, and record an outcome. No administrator access is required.

<Callout kind="info">
  You need reviewer access to at least one workspace. If your dashboard is empty, ask an administrator to assign you a workspace.
</Callout>

## Triage a finding

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Critical queue" icon="gauge" title-type="h3">
    Start with the highest tier. Critical means sensitive data is broadly reachable right now.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Read the finding" icon="file-search" title-type="h3">
    Check the label, the Sensitivity and Exposure breakdown, and the rationale that explains why the asset was flagged.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Decide an action" icon="list-checks" title-type="h3">
    Reduce sharing scope, apply a protection policy, or mark the finding as expected behavior with a note.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Record the outcome" icon="check" title-type="h3">
    Log what you did. The finding history feeds your audit trail and reporting.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Triage outcomes

| Outcome | When to use it | Effect |
|---------|----------------|--------|
| Remediated | You reduced exposure or applied a control | Finding closes, exposure drops |
| Accepted risk | Exposure is justified and approved | Finding closes with a documented reason |
| Expected behavior | The finding is by design | Finding is suppressed for that pattern |
| Needs help | You cannot resolve it alone | Escalates to an administrator |

<Callout kind="alert">
  Do not mark a Critical finding as expected behavior without an approver. See the [Expected Behaviour](/known-issues/expected-behaviour) page for the difference between expected behavior and a real exposure.
</Callout>
